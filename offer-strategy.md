**Adjacent services I provide (à la carte, not part of the three-tier offer):**

These are bundled separately for clients who need the full stack (see "Drew context" below). The three-tier offer is **personal branding only.**

## 2. Pricing Rationale — Why These Numbers Hold

Before laying out the tiers, the why. The pricing isn't pulled from the sky — it's anchored to what these services cost in the open market, and to what the prospect would have to spend to replace me piece-by-piece.

### Comparable market rates

| Service | Solo / freelance | Agency rate |
|---|---|---|
| Custom CRM build (one-time) | $80k–150k | $150k–300k |
| Custom CRM maintenance | $2k–5k/mo | $5k–15k/mo |
| Social media management (1 account) | $500–2,500/mo | $1,500–10,000/mo |
| Content production + filming (weekly) | $2k–6k/mo | $4k–15k/mo |
| Posting + community management | $500–1,500/mo | $1,500–5,000/mo |

### What this means for the three-tier offer

The DFY tier at $3,997/mo represents roughly **40-60% of what the same level of service would cost from a full-service agency**. That's the gap that lets me win producers off the fence: I'm the in-office, half-price alternative to bringing on a Nashville-based content agency with a 6-month onboarding ramp.

The DIY tier at $397/mo is a **playbook-and-tools price point** — competitive with course-and-community offerings in the insurance niche, but with personal relevance because I'm down the hall and shooting other agents in the same office.

DWY at $1,997/mo is the middle slot for producers who want the system + occasional production help but aren't ready to hand the keys over.

## 3. The RCTC Framework

Every tier is built around the same content framework. The framework is the product — the tiers vary in how much of the production I take off the prospect's plate.

**RCTC = Reach · Trust · Connection · Conversion**

Every reel an agent posts should serve one of these four purposes. The split between phone-shot and pro-camera-shot content runs along this line:

| Quadrant | Purpose | Camera | Who shoots | Volume |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Reach** | Get in front of new eyes. Talking-head opinion content, mass-produced. | Phone | Prospect | High — 3-5 per week |
| **Trust** | Show they're real, working their craft. Live calls, in-office moments, ride-alongs. | Pro camera | Me | Medium — 1-2 per week |
| **Connection** | Personality, relatability, behind-the-scenes. Lifestyle-adjacent. | Phone | Prospect | Medium — 2-3 per week |
| **Conversion** | Direct CTA — book a call, recruit, sell. Polished and intentional. | Pro camera | Me | Low — 1-2 per week |

### Why phone for Reach and Connection

These are the high-volume quadrants. The platform algorithm rewards raw, native content from inside the user's account. My pro camera footage is a supplement, not the main fuel. **Any agent who refuses to shoot their own phone content is not a fit for any tier — the system doesn't work without their volume.** I'll say this on the sales call.

### Why pro camera for Trust and Conversion

These quadrants need production value because they're the conversion engine. A polished sit-down interview, an over-the-shoulder shot of a live close, a cinematic recruiting CTA — these need to look intentional, and that's where my camera earns its keep.

## 4. The Three-Tier Offer

### Tier 1 — DIY · $397/month

**For:** Solo producers who want the system but aren't ready to invest in production. Newer agents, side-hustle producers, anyone testing the waters.

**What they get:**
- The full RCTC playbook (framework explained, examples, why it works)
- Hook bank — 50+ proven hooks specific to insurance content
- Script templates for each RCTC quadrant
- Posting schedule template (when to drop what, how to space the quadrants)
- Monthly group coaching call (60 minutes, all DIY clients together)
- Private community for feedback and accountability

**What they do:**
- Shoot all four quadrants themselves (phone for Reach/Connection, phone or DSLR for Trust/Conversion)
- Edit, post, and engage themselves

**What's not included:**
- Any filming, editing, posting, or 1:1 coaching
- Custom scripts written for them
- Performance reviews

**Sales line:** "I'll teach you the system other producers are paying me to run for them. You do the work, you keep all the upside."

---

### Tier 2 — Done With You · $1,997/month

**For:** Producers who can shoot but want structure, accountability, and occasional production help.

**Everything in DIY, plus:**
- Biweekly 1:1 strategy call (45 minutes — review last two weeks, plan next two)
- Unlimited script review — they draft, I tighten
- One in-person filming day per quarter (4 hours — I bring the camera, we capture 90 days of Trust + Conversion content in one session)
- Editing for up to 4 of their phone-shot reels per month
- Quarterly analytics review with strategic adjustments

**What they do:**
- Shoot Reach + Connection on phone every week
- Post + engage themselves
- Show up prepared for the quarterly shoot day

**Sales line:** "We build the system together. You're still the operator, but you're not figuring it out alone."

---

### Tier 3 — Done For You · $3,997/month

**For:** Producers who want their personal brand to grow without thinking about content production. Closers, recruiters, agency owners with bandwidth pinned on selling.

**Everything in DWY, plus:**
- Weekly in-person filming (1-2 hours — catching live calls, in-office moments, ride-alongs, interviews — Trust + Conversion content captured with my camera as their job happens)
- Full editing for every phone-shot reel they send me (no monthly cap)
- Posting management — I handle all uploads, captions, hashtags, scheduling
- Monthly hook + topic refresh — fresh prompts so they're never staring at a phone wondering what to film
- Monthly performance review with KPIs (reach, saves, follows, DMs, recruiting conversions)
- Minimum 8-12 published reels per month (combined output across all four quadrants)

**What they do:**
- Shoot Reach + Connection on phone (most weeks)
- Show up for the weekly filming window
- Reply to DMs and comments themselves (I never impersonate them — authenticity is the whole point)

**Sales line:** "You make insurance happen. I make the internet think you're a celebrity doing it."

## 6. How to Pitch Each Tier in the Office

The three-tier offer creates a clean conversation with anyone in the office:

### Conversation flow

1. **"Are you serious about building a personal brand on social media?"**
   - No → Walk away. Don't sell to non-believers.
   - Yes → Continue.

2. **"Are you willing to shoot 3-5 phone reels per week, every week?"**
   - No → Not a fit for any tier. The system requires volume from them.
   - Yes → Continue.

3. **"How much of the production do you want to handle yourself?"**
   - Everything → DIY ($397). Hand them the playbook.
   - Some, but I want help and accountability → DWY ($1,997).
   - As little as possible → DFY ($3,997).

### Common objections and responses

**"$4k/month is a lot."**
→ "It is. It's also less than half what an Atlanta or Nashville content agency would charge you for the same output, and they don't sit in your office. The agents on this tier are running insurance — you're not running content. That's the trade."

**"Can't I just do DIY and figure it out?"**
→ "Absolutely you can, and that's why DIY exists. Just know what the cost is: most agents who try DIY abandon it within 60 days because they can't sustain the volume alone. DWY exists for the same agent six months in, when they realize the volume's the bottleneck."

**"Why is DFY 10x more expensive than DIY?"**
→ "Because DFY is 10x more work for me. DIY is leveraged — I record the playbook once, dozens of agents can use it. DFY is custom — every shoot day is yours, every edit is yours, every post is yours. The price reflects the time."

**"Can I try DFY for one month?"**
→ "No. Two reasons. First, the system needs 90 days minimum to compound — one month of reels won't show results yet, and you'd quit thinking it didn't work. Second, my filming calendar locks 90 days out. If I take a one-month trial, I'm turning down a quarter of work from someone serious."

## 7. The Bigger Strategy

**Why having three tiers matters even if I only sell DFY:**

Right now, my income is dependent on Drew. If Drew leaves, the business cuts in half overnight. That's an unacceptable risk for a service business.

The three-tier offer is **risk distribution**. Goals:
- 1 DFY client (Drew, plus maybe one more eventually) → $4k/mo each
- 3-5 DWY clients → $2k/mo each = $6-10k/mo
- 10-20 DIY clients → $400/mo each = $4-8k/mo
- **Total potential revenue: $14-22k/mo from tiers alone**, before counting CRM + website work for Drew

This positioning also flips the conversation with Drew. Right now he might feel I'm uniquely dependent on him. With a multi-tier business running across the office, **I'm running an in-office content service that Drew happens to be the biggest client of.** Way better seat at the table.
