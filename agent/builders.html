Builders

Build your team.

Building a team is a different skill than producing. Your job shifts from closing policies to setting the standards, asking the right questions on day one, and holding new agents to the cadence below. Use this page as your reference for every new hire you bring on.

Set the standards

01

Consistency

Hold your team to the same start time, end time, and dial discipline every day. Their consistency mirrors yours.

02

Standards

Don't lower the bar to retain people. Anyone who can't meet the standard hurts the team's culture more than they help your override.

03

Confidence

Your team mirrors your tone. Walk into the dial room expecting big weeks and they'll show up the same way.

Discovery questions

Ask every new hire in their first conversation. Their answers are the anchor you'll come back to when they hit a tough week.

"

What are your goals for yourself?

Listen for specifics like income, lifestyle, or time freedom. If they give a generic answer, dig until you hear something concrete you can hold them to.

"

What are you hoping to get out of this?

This is the why you'll bring back when the work gets hard. Write it down where you'll see it on coaching calls.

Setting expectations

Communicate these on day one and reinforce them weekly. Vague expectations lead to vague results.

  • Consistent schedule. Same start time, end time, every day. Make sure your team knows when the dial begins and that the schedule isn't optional.
  • On Zoom, unmuted, camera on. Trainings only work when agents are present. No black squares, no half-attention. Set the rule and enforce it.
  • Walk the script together. Sit with every new agent and go line by line. They should understand the purpose of each line before they ever touch a phone.

Onboarding checklist

Walk every new hire through these phases in order, from exam day to first dial. Verify each step before you let them move to the next.

1
Pre-exam

Lock in your exam.

  • Schedule your exam. Two-week deadline. Have them send proof (screenshot or text confirmation) to admin.
  • Claim your free course voucher.
  • Pass the exam.
2
Post-exam

Apply for your license.

  • Create a dedicated work Gmail. Format: firstlast.ffl@gmail.com. Text the new address to admin.
  • Apply for your state license.
  • Schedule fingerprints. If their state requires it. Schedule after the license application is in.
3
While you wait

Build the foundation.

  • Join the team Discord and GroupMe. Links live on Quick Links.
  • Complete boot camp.
  • Attend all daily trainings and meet with manager nightly.
  • Study the sales script and rebuttals.
4
Post-license approval

Launch and dial.

  • Check for your National Producer Number (NPN). Usually available about a week after license approval.
  • Purchase E&O insurance. Select the lowest option (Option A). Send proof to admin.
  • Submit a voided check. For commission deposit setup. Send to admin.
  • Complete contracting with carriers. Schedule a Zoom appointment with admin to walk through it.
  • Start dialing and training. Use paper applications until your carrier approvals come through.

Once a new agent clears the checklist, their day-to-day cadence lives on the Training page. Each week unlocks for them as they check off the previous one. Use the Admin page to track every agent's progress in one place.
